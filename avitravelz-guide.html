Award Travel, Step by Step
✈️
Flying for (almost) free is real. Here's how it works.
Every time you swipe your credit card, you earn points. Most people let those points expire or redeem them for a $10 Amazon coupon. A small group of people uses those same points to fly business class to Europe for $50 in taxes. This guide teaches you how to be in that second group.
The short version

Banks partner with airlines and hotels. When you spend on a credit card, you earn the bank's points. You can then move those points to the airline, who gives you miles. You use those miles to book award seats, flights at a fraction of the cash price.

The key insight: a business class seat to Tokyo that costs $5,000 in cash might cost 90,000 miles + $80 in taxes. Those miles came from everyday spending: groceries, gas, dining out. No tricks. No loopholes. Just knowing how the system works.

Is this for you?
✅ This works if you...
•Pay your credit card in full every month
•Already spend on groceries, dining, gas, and subscriptions
•Have good enough credit to qualify for premium cards
•Are willing to spend 30 minutes learning the system
🚫 This does NOT work if you...
•Carry a balance or pay interest on your cards
•Spend more than you normally would just to earn points
•Miss or make late payments regularly
•Are already struggling with credit card debt
Read this before you open any new card
Credit card interest kills every point you earn. If you carry a balance and pay 20–30% interest, no amount of free travel makes up for that. This system only works if you treat your credit card like a debit card. Spend what you have, pay it off in full when the statement closes, every single month. One month of interest charges on a $5,000 balance will cost you more than the flight you were trying to earn. If that's not you right now, bookmark this guide and come back when it is. No judgment.
