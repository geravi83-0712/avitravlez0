Points Transfer Simulator
This is a practice run. Walk through how to move your credit card points to an airline so when you do it for real, you already know the steps.
Heads up before you start These screens are not identical to the real bank portals. Layouts, button names, and menus change over time. But the overall process is the same across all of them. Use this to get comfortable with the flow, not to memorize every pixel.
Before you start (real life)
1
You need a loyalty account with the airline you are transferring to. Most are free to join. Sign up on the airline's website before you start.
2
The first time you transfer to a new airline, your bank will ask you to link your loyalty account. You enter your account number and name. After that, it stays saved and you skip this step.

What you will practice

1
See your points dashboard and understand which cards can transfer to airlines and which ones cannot.
2
For Chase: combine points from Freedom cards into your Sapphire first. This is a step most people miss.
3
Pick an airline partner, enter your loyalty account number, and choose how many points to move.
4
Review the transfer and confirm. In real life this is permanent, so the review step matters.

Heads up: Amex charges a transfer fee for Delta
Amex is the only bank that passes a federal excise tax to cardholders when transferring to U.S. airlines. The fee is $0.0006 per point, capped at $99. It only applies to Delta and JetBlue. Every other Amex partner is free.
$42

$0
Fee on all other partners
Pay with your card, not your points. Paying the fee with points only gets you 0.5 cents per point, which is terrible value. Use the card instead and save your points for flights.
Pro tip: avoid the fee entirely. You can book Delta flights using Air France/KLM Flying Blue, Virgin Atlantic Flying Club, or AeroMexico Club Premier, all Amex partners. None of those charge an excise fee.
Before transferring, go to the airline's website and confirm the award seat is actually available. Transfers cannot be reversed, so verify first.
